**How do I identify and fix the hidden problems that are making customers leave my website without buying?**

Great question! As someone who's analyzed thousands of customer journeys, I can tell you that most business owners focus on the obvious stuff (broken links, slow pages) while missing the real conversion killers: hidden friction points.

Think of it like pebbles in your shoe. One pebble? Annoying but manageable. Five pebbles? You're taking the shoe off and going elsewhere. Your website works the same way — small frustrations compound into deal-breaking experiences.

**The biggest hidden friction points I see:**

1. **Information gaps** — Missing pricing details, vague product descriptions, unclear next steps
2. **Trust deficits** — No testimonials, missing contact info, or sterile corporate speak
3. **Decision fatigue** — Too many choices or steps overwhelming customers
4. **Mobile unfriendliness** — Clunky mobile experience (60%+ of traffic is mobile!)
5. **Unclear value** — Visitors can't quickly understand what you do and why it matters

**Here's my simple detection method:**

Walk through your own buying process monthly. Start from Google search, go all the way to purchase completion. Time yourself. Count clicks. Note every moment of confusion or frustration.

Better yet? Watch someone else try to buy from you. Their hesitations reveal your friction points better than any analytics.

**The fix isn't just removing problems — it's replacing them with trust signals.** Instead of just eliminating a confusing pricing page, create clear cost breakdowns with no hidden fees. Replace generic contact forms with multiple contact options and expected response times.

Don't try to fix everything at once. Pick one friction point, design a trust-building alternative, implement it, measure results, then move to the next one.

The goal isn't perfection — it's progression. Every friction point you transform becomes a competitive advantage.
