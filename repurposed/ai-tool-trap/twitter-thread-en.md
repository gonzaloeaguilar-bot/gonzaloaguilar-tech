2/🧵 Most of us discover AI tools backwards:

• See a cool LinkedIn demo
• Sign up for "free trial" 
• Pay monthly fees for tools we barely use

The average small business wastes $3K-$8K annually on unused software subscriptions.

3/🧵 The Three Questions Framework (ask in this exact order):

1️⃣ What SPECIFIC problem am I solving?
2️⃣ How will I measure success? 
3️⃣ What's my backup plan if this doesn't work?

Most AI tools will fail at least one question — saving you from expensive mistakes.

4/🧵 Question 1: Get specific or go broke.

❌ "I want AI to help with marketing"
✅ "I spend 4 hours every Tuesday writing social posts, need that time for client calls"

If you can't explain the problem in one sentence to your grandmother, you don't understand it well enough to buy a solution.

5/🧵 Question 2: Pick your number.

Before you buy, write down the metric that would make this purchase obviously worth it:

• "Save me 2 hours per week"
• "Reduce response time from 4 hours to 1 hour"  
• "Generate 5 new qualified leads monthly"

No number = not ready to buy.

6/🧵 Question 3: Always have Plan B.

• How do you export your data?
• What's the cancellation process?
• Can you still run your business without this tool?

I've seen companies pay $200/month for years just because switching felt overwhelming. Don't get trapped.

7/🧵 Pro tip: Do the 30-day reality check.

Before buying ANY AI tool, manually track your "problem" for 30 days.

You can't improve what you don't measure. This exercise alone eliminates 80% of unnecessary AI purchases.

Most "problems" aren't as bad as we think.

8/🧵 The framework isn't about avoiding AI — it's about choosing tools that actually grow your business.

Start small. Pick one frustrating process. Apply the three questions.
