**How do I know if an AI tool is worth the investment for my small business?**

As someone who's helped dozens of small businesses navigate AI adoption, I can tell you that 67% of small businesses abandon their AI tools within six months. The issue isn't that AI doesn't work—it's that most business owners buy solutions before understanding their problems.

Here's my Three Questions Framework that's saved my clients thousands in wasted subscriptions:

**1. What specific problem am I solving?**
Don't say "I want AI to help with marketing." That's too vague. Instead: "I spend 4 hours every Tuesday writing social media posts, and I need that time for client calls." If you can't explain the problem to your grandmother in one sentence, you don't understand it well enough to buy a solution.

**2. How will I measure success?**
Pick a real number. "This should save me 2 hours per week" or "Customer response time should drop from 4 hours to 1 hour." Without clear metrics, you'll keep paying for tools that feel useful but don't improve your bottom line.

**3. What's my backup plan?**
Always have an exit strategy. Can you export your data? What's the cancellation process? Could you still run your business if the tool disappeared tomorrow?

Most AI tools fail at least one of these questions, which saves you from expensive mistakes.

Before buying anything, track your "problem" manually for 30 days. You can't improve what you don't measure, and this exercise alone eliminates 80% of unnecessary purchases.

The goal isn't to avoid AI—it's to choose tools that actually move your business forward.
