# Carousel Outline: The Anxiety Map Framework

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
**Your customers are scared of you 😰**
Not personally — but every buying decision triggers a cascade of worries that kills sales faster than bad reviews.

**Slide 2:**
**Most businesses build first, research later**
You create products based on what makes sense to YOU, then wonder why customers don't convert. Plot twist: you're solving the wrong puzzle.

**Slide 3:**
**Customer anxiety = mental static drowning your sales message**
Think restaurant with no prices, nervous waiters, kitchen arguments. Even if it smells amazing, you're walking out. That's anxiety in action.

**Slide 4:**
**The hidden cost: you're running business with parking brake on**
Anxious customers don't just leave quietly — they tell friends about your "sketchy website." You're paying for marketing that generates anti-marketing.

**Slide 5:**
**Introducing the Anxiety Map Framework**
3 phases: Discovery (what are they worried about?), Categorization (when do worries spike?), Prioritization (which fears kill conversions?).

**Slide 6:**
**Phase 1: Discovery - Stop guessing, start asking**
Survey recent customers: "What almost stopped you from buying?" Mine support data for repeat questions. The answers will shock you.

**Slide 7:**
**Phase 2: Categorization - 6 anxiety types**
Financial, Performance, Security, Social, Process, Support. Each hits at different customer journey stages. Map yours to see patterns.

**Slide 8:**
**Phase 3: Prioritization - Not all fears are equal**
Plot anxieties by impact vs. effort to fix. Focus on high-impact, low-effort wins first. Quick example: clear shipping times on product pages.

**Slide 9:**
**From map to action: Build anxiety-aware experiences**
Lead with reassurance on high-anxiety pages. Anticipate questions before they're asked. Make canceling easy (paradoxically reduces hesitation).

**Slide 10 (CTA):**
**Ready to stop losing customers to fear?**
