1/8 🧵 Your customers are scared of you.

Not personally — they're scared of making the wrong choice, wasting money, or looking foolish. Every buying decision triggers anxiety that kills sales faster than bad reviews.

Most businesses build first, research later. Here's why that's backwards:

2/8 Customer anxiety = the mental static drowning out your sales message.

Think: restaurant with no prices, nervous waiters, kitchen arguments. Even if the food smells amazing, you're walking out.

Your customers don't know your business is legit yet. You do. They don't. Bridge that gap.

3/8 The hidden cost of ignoring customer fears:

• Pay for traffic that won't convert (80% leave worried)
• Generate negative word-of-mouth unknowingly  
• Miss real optimization opportunities
• Waste months fixing the wrong problems

You're running your business with the parking brake on.

4/8 Enter the Anxiety Map framework — 3 phases:

🔍 DISCOVERY: What are they worried about?
📊 CATEGORIZATION: When do worries spike?  
🎯 PRIORITIZATION: Which fears hurt business most?

It's GPS for customer emotions. Don't build experiences blind.

5/8 Discovery tactics that actually work:

• Survey recent customers: "What almost stopped you from buying?"
• Mine support data for repeat questions
• Track sales objections systematically
• Study drop-off points in your funnel
• Research competitor complaints

Stop guessing. Start asking.

6/8 Categorize anxiety into 6 buckets:

💰 Financial (hidden fees, worth it?)
⚡ Performance (will this work?)
🔒 Security (can I trust this?)
👥 Social (will I look foolish?)
🔄 Process (how long, how complex?)
🆘 Support (what if something breaks?)

Pattern recognition > random fixes.

7/8 Prioritization matrix magic:

Plot each anxiety: Impact vs Effort to fix

Quick wins = High impact, low effort
(Example: Adding shipping estimates vs redesigning entire checkout)

Address fears that affect 80% of customers before ones affecting 10%.

8/8 Don't let another prospect slip away because you're solving the wrong problem.

Map their anxiety first. Build what customers actually want to buy.
