**Question: How do I know if my small business is actually ready for AI, or if I should wait?**

Great question! I see so many small business owners jumping into AI because of all the hype, only to waste money and frustrate their teams. The truth? Most businesses aren't ready yet, and that's totally fine.

Here's my quick reality check — I call it the Three Questions Framework:

**1. Can you clearly describe what specific problem you want AI to solve?** If your answer is "AI will just make everything better," pump the brakes. You need a concrete pain point, like "I spend 3 hours daily on customer service emails" or "I manually schedule all our social media posts."

**2. Do you have organized data and processes?** AI needs good inputs to work. If your customer info is scattered across sticky notes and random spreadsheets, AI can't help you. Think of AI as a smart employee who still needs organized information to do their job.

**3. Is your team ready for change?** This is where most businesses stumble. AI changes workflows, not just tasks. If your team is already overwhelmed or resistant to new tools, adding AI creates more problems.

Beyond these basics, you also need documented processes (you can explain how key activities happen step-by-step), clear success metrics (you know what "good" looks like in numbers), and budget cushion for experimentation.

Red flags to wait: you're behind on basic business practices, your team struggles with current technology, or you're hoping AI will solve fundamental business model problems.

Start small when you're ready. Focus on one specific problem. There's no prize for being first — there's huge value in being effective.

Tranquilo — build your foundation first, then AI will actually help you.
