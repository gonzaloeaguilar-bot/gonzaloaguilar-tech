## "Is Your Business Actually Ready for AI? An Honest Checklist"

---

### Stat 1 — Gartner: AI projects fail without AI-ready data

**Data point:** Gartner predicts that through 2026, organizations will abandon 60% of AI projects that lack AI-ready data. Separately, 63% of organizations either do not have — or are unsure whether they have — the right data management practices to support AI.

**Source:** Gartner, "Lack of AI-Ready Data Puts AI Projects at Risk," February 2025. https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-02-26-lack-of-ai-ready-data-puts-ai-projects-at-risk

**Where to insert:** "Technology Infrastructure" section, under "Your Data Is Centralized and Accessible." Turns the general advice into an evidence-backed warning.

**Exact sentence to add (after "AI can't connect the dots to help you."):**
> Gartner's February 2025 research found that 63% of organizations don't have — or don't know if they have — the data management practices AI requires, and predicts that through 2026, organizations will abandon 60% of AI projects that lack AI-ready data. Messy data isn't just inconvenient; it's the leading predictor of failure.

---

### Stat 2 — GenAI project abandonment after POC

**Data point:** Gartner predicted that at least 30% of generative AI projects will be abandoned after proof of concept by end of 2025, due to poor data quality, inadequate risk controls, escalating costs, or unclear business value.

**Source:** Gartner press release, July 2024. https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2024-07-29-gartner-predicts-30-percent-of-generative-ai-projects-will-be-abandoned-after-proof-of-concept-by-end-of-2025

**Where to insert:** "Red Flags That You Should Wait" section, as supporting data for why jumping in unprepared is genuinely costly — not just uncomfortable.

**Exact sentence to add (before or after the bullet list of red flags):**
> This isn't a hypothetical warning. Gartner predicted that at least 30% of generative AI projects would be abandoned after proof of concept by end of 2025 — the primary causes being poor data quality, unclear business value, and underestimated costs. Readiness isn't a nice-to-have; it's what separates the 30% that get abandoned from the ones that stick.

---

### Stat 3 — Only 6% of AI users are high performers

**Data point:** 88% of organizations now use AI in at least one business function — but only 6% qualify as "high performers" seeing significant bottom-line impact. 39% see any EBIT impact at all.

**Source:** McKinsey Global AI Survey, November 2025. https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

**Where to insert:** Opening section (after "jumping into AI without being ready is like trying to build a second floor before your foundation is solid"), to anchor the readiness argument with hard adoption data.

**Exact sentence to add (as a new paragraph after the opening warning):**
> The numbers tell a brutal story: McKinsey's 2025 Global AI Survey found that 88% of organizations now use AI in some capacity — but only 6% qualify as high performers seeing meaningful bottom-line impact. The gap between "using AI" and "benefiting from AI" is where most small businesses are stuck.
